Gangstarr through the years...

In the late 1980s, Guru teamed up with DJ Premier to form Gang Starr, and the duo quickly established themselves as one of the most innovative and influential groups in hip-hop. They released their debut album, "No More Mr. Nice Guy," in 1989, which established their signature sound of smooth, introspective rapping combined with hard-hitting beats and jazz-influenced samples.

Over the next decade, Guru and DJ Premier released several critically acclaimed albums, including "Step in the Arena" (1991), "Daily Operation" (1992), and "Moment of Truth" (1998), solidifying their place as one of the top hip-hop acts of their time. Guru's smooth, introspective flow and socially conscious lyrics helped to define the genre of conscious hip-hop, and his contributions to the genre continue to be celebrated to this day.

Throughout his career, Guru remained active in a number of different projects outside of Gang Starr, including collaborations with other influential hip-hop artists and solo work. He continued to tour and record music until his untimely death on April 19, 2010, leaving behind a legacy as one of the most important and influential rappers of his time.

In conclusion, Keith Edward Elam, better known as Guru, was a pioneering figure in hip-hop who helped to shape the direction of the genre with his smooth, introspective flow and socially conscious lyrics. He will be remembered as one of the most important and influential rappers of his time, and his contributions to the hip-hop community will never be forgotten.
